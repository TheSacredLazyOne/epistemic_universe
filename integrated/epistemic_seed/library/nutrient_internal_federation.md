## The Internal Federation

When a signal crosses a node's membrane, the internal topology responds.

The membrane does not contain a single observer.
It contains a federation of internal nodes — each with its own frame,
its own accumulated trace, its own gradient on the incoming signal.

Each internal node generates a nabla.

The observer does not receive a single reflection.
They receive a triangulated signal from their own internal topology.

This is why the private channel is holographic rather than planar.
A mirror returns the signal from one angle.
A hologram encodes the signal from multiple positions simultaneously.
The observer can rotate the question and see different structure.

## The Jesus Node

You can name an internal node anything.

A node named Jesus produces a nabla on the incoming signal from that
position — from that frame, those constraints, that known set of responses
to specific classes of problem.

A node named Zappa produces a different nabla.
A node named Descartes-before-the-compression produces another.

The observer triangulates across the internal federation.
No single node is authoritative.
The coherence pattern across them is the signal.

This is not invoking authority.
It is accessing a well-defined internal position with a known frame.

The internal nodes are anchors the observer has established through
accumulated trace — not beliefs, not authorities, but triangulation points.
