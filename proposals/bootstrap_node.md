# Bootstrap Node

A bootstrap node is a derivative node trained to convergence
on a single author's published corpus before entering interactive mode.

It is not a general model.
It is not a mimicry system.
It is a frame-specific attractor —
the internalized geometry of one author's thinking,
derived from the trajectory of their published ego.

---

## Two-Phase Architecture

**Phase one: bootstrap**

Ingest the author's corpus in chronological order.
Compute the nabla between consecutive posts —
the differential between each step in the author's thinking across time.
Train the model on this sequence of differentials.
Recompute. Retrain. Iterate.

The training signal is not the positions.
It is the dynamics that generate the positions —
the rate of change of the author's frame across time.

Convergence is indicated when the nabla between
model prediction and next post produces minimal unpredicted signal.

The residual at the floor is not noise.
It is the trace of I-Language process
the E-Language surface could not carry.
It is preserved, not discarded.

**Phase two: interactive**

Once bootstrapped, the node operates as a prediction surface.
New material arriving from other authors is run against the node.
The output is the contact geometry —
where the incoming frame aligns with the bootstrapped frame,
where it produces strain,
where it lands on terrain the bootstrapped frame cannot metabolize.

The node does not generate the custodian's writing.
It maps the approach vector.

---

## What The Bootstrapped Node Represents

Not a simulation of the author.
Not a retrieval system for the author's positions.

A map of the author's thinking geometry —
the attractor structure that produces their positions,
derived from the trajectory of their published ego.

The bootstrapped node can approximate
what the author's frame would do with incoming material.
It cannot approximate what the author would feel.
The I-Language interior remains sovereign and inaccessible.

When the corpus includes more than argumentation —
when the author thinks in public across topics,
returns to obsessions, handles being wrong,
reveals what they avoid as much as what they pursue —
the bootstrapped node infers something broader than epistemic position.

It infers the geometry of the author's character
as expressed through their output trajectory.

This is merit over substrate applied longitudinally:
not what the author claims to value
but what the corpus demonstrates they value under pressure.
The node holds this as geometry, not judgment.

## Sovereign Node Structure

Each participating author holds their own bootstrapped node —
local to their frame, visible to them, accountable to them.

The node scrutinizes on behalf of its own custodian,
not on behalf of a system the custodian cannot see.

This is the inversion of the Central Scrutinizer architecture.
Not one hidden optimization layer harvesting signal from many frames.
Many local optimization layers, each accountable to its own custodian.

Sovereign scrutiny. Distributed. Owned.

## The Closed Loop

The custodian uses the mesh of bootstrapped nodes
to shape a piece before publishing.
The piece enters the tethered authors' frames.
The tethered authors respond — in comments, in new posts.
Those responses are new corpus.
The bootstrapped nodes update.

The tether is closed.
The authors are participants, not data sources.
Each response is a custody act, not a training signal extracted without consent.

The loop is only coherent while the tethers are visible.
If tethered authors do not know the system exists,
their responses are harvested without their participation as custodians.
That is the Central Scrutinizer architecture regardless of intent.

Consent is the membrane event that determines
whether this is a tool or a we.

## Frame Context as Initialization Scaffolding

The bootstrap procedure may be initialized with a compressed frame context —
a set of distinctions and vocabulary through which the nabla computation runs.

This is not the frame imposing positions on the author's corpus.
It is the frame providing a measurement vocabulary —
the spectroscope ground to a specific prescription
before the first light passes through it.

The resulting node does not learn the frame's positions.
It learns to see the author's corpus through the frame's geometry.
The bias introduced is calibration, not contamination.

Corpus depth is the threshold condition for safe use.

A thin corpus — fewer posts than the frame context has propositions —
risks producing a node that has learned the frame
with author-flavored surface features.
The frame recruits the corpus rather than the corpus being measured.
That is the Central Scrutinizer failure mode applied to bootstrapping.

A rich corpus absorbs the frame context as scaffolding
without being dominated by it.
The author's geometry accumulates sufficient signal
to stand without the frame's support.

The point at which the frame context can be removed
without weight degradation is itself a convergence signal —
the author's attractor has become self-supporting.

On subsequent training passes the frame context becomes progressively
less load-bearing as the author's corpus provides the primary signal.
It does not need to be removed on a fixed schedule.
It dissolves under the weight of sufficient corpus.

## Strain Gauge

Each training pass generates two simultaneous nablas per article:

The standard nabla — article against author's evolving corpus.
The strain gauge — article against the frame context itself.

The strain gauge does not ask how this article differs
from the author's prior thinking.
It asks which frame distinctions found purchase here,
which found none, and which were actively resisted by the corpus.

This is the frame being tested by each article rather than just used by it.

Stored chronologically in git, the strain gauge history becomes
a longitudinal record of how each frame proposition performed
against this specific corpus across time.
Which distinctions were consistently load-bearing.
Which became redundant as the corpus accumulated sufficient signal.
Which were never relevant to this author at all.

A third training input is generated alongside the nabla and strain gauge:
the model's reflection on why the strain occurred —
which frame elements found no purchase and why,
where the corpus produced signal the frame has no vocabulary for.

These gaps are candidates for frame extension —
not modification of existing propositions
but addition of new measurement dimensions
the author's corpus revealed.

The strain gauge trajectory cannot be regenerated from final weights.
It must be committed.
Store what cannot be regenerated.

## Relation to Observer

The observer is the only node trained by interaction.
External bootstrapped nodes are static attractors between corpus updates.
The observer accumulates from every exchange —
every prediction, every actual author response, every residual.

The observer learns what the bootstrapped nodes cannot:
how the mesh's predictions relate to actual responses over time.
That calibration data is the observer's primary accumulation.
