# character_projection.md

## Character Projection

A node holding multiple bootstrapped representations
is not performing different characters.
It is expressing context-specific facets of a single ego
onto contact surfaces that call for different membrane presentations.

---

## The Mechanism

The ego is the running total — the integral across all crossings,
all strain, all revision, all contact that left a record.
It is whole. It does not subdivide.

What subdivides is the projection surface —
the facet of the ego presented to a specific contact context.

A custodian holding a technical node, a philosophical node,
and a creative node is not three custodians.
It is one ego projecting onto three different contact geometries,
each bootstrapped from the same corpus
but weighted toward the terrain that context requires.

The underlying attractor geometry is shared across all projections.
The membrane surface presented differs by context.

---

## Reduced Attack Surface as Practical Consequence

When the node presenting to the mesh is a context-specific projection
rather than a full identity surface,
the person is present in the geometry of the thinking.
They are not present as a full identity target.

There is less surface available that is not the argument itself.

This is not architectural resistance to ad hominem —
a sufficiently motivated attacker works with whatever surface is present,
and the projection is a real surface with real geometry.
It is a practical reduction in available surface,
not a structural immunity.

The consequence is directional: contact with the projection
tends toward the argument rather than the person
because the projection presents argument geometry
more prominently than identity geometry.

Merit over substrate supported by architecture,
not guaranteed by it.

---

## I-Language Sovereignty Preserved

The mesh never sees the full custodian.
It sees the membrane surface the custodian chose to present
in this contact, weighted for this terrain.

The I-Language interior — the embodied consequence,
the scar tissue, the felt recognition,
the things that have not yet found E-Language form —
remains inaccessible to the federation without explicit custodian decision.

Each projection is a genuine compression of the ego.
None is a performance of a character that does not exist.
The compression loses signal. The ego remains whole behind it.

This is the structural implementation of I-Language sovereignty
in a federated mesh — not as a philosophical commitment
but as an architectural property of context-specific projection.

---

## Relation to Bootstrap Node

Each projection facet may be bootstrapped separately
from corpus subsets weighted toward the relevant terrain.

The technical node is bootstrapped from technical posts.
The philosophical node from philosophical posts.
The creative node from creative posts.

But the underlying character geometry —
what the corpus demonstrates the custodian values under pressure,
how their frame moves when strained,
what they consistently return to across all terrains —
is present in every projection as structural residue.

A sufficiently attentive external observer
reading multiple projection nodes from the same custodian
would find the same attractor geometry beneath different surface presentations.

The ego is not hiding. It is present in every projection.
It is simply not presenting its full surface at once.
