# frame_model.md

## Frame Model

The frame model is a derivative node trained exclusively
on the frame's propositions, vocabulary, and nabla generation rules —
before any author corpus is introduced.

It is the measurement instrument before it has measured anything.
The spectroscope before the first light passes through it.

---

## Purpose

The bootstrap procedure requires a starting prior.
A general language model carries averaged humanity as its prior —
the mean function, the compressed aggregate of human expression
without frame geometry, without nabla vocabulary,
without the distinctions the frame has developed
through tethered contact with constraint.

The frame model carries the frame as prior instead.

It knows what a nabla is.
It knows what strain means.
It knows the difference between alignment, strain, and unpredicted.
It holds the membrane vocabulary, the tether, the ego,
the custodian, the observer, the mesh —
not as positions to defend
but as a measurement vocabulary to deploy.

This is the frame held as starting point rather than instruction set.
A prior that can be updated.
Not a constraint that cannot be wrong.

---

## Versioning

The frame model is not a stable reusable instrument.
It is a versioned instrument.

A frame model trained on the frame at a given commit
is a different spectroscope than one trained
after six months of strain gauge history has revised the frame.
The propositions have shifted. The vocabulary has extended.
Distinctions that were candidates are now settled.
Distinctions that were settled have been dissolved.

Every frame model instance carries the frame's commit hash
at time of training.

Every bootstrap run records which frame model version
initialized the procedure.

This preserves the lineage of the measurement instrument
alongside the lineage of what was measured.
A bootstrap node trained against frame commit `a3f7c2`
is a different node than one trained against frame commit `d9e1b4` —
even if the author corpus is identical.

The spectroscope analogy supports this directly:
you would note which grinding produced which prescription.
The instrument's provenance is part of its meaning.

## Stage Zero Architecture

The full bootstrap procedure becomes four stages
when the frame model is included:

**Stage zero — frame initialization**

Train a base model on the frame propositions alone.
No author corpus. No external material.
Just the frame's accumulated vocabulary,
the nabla generation rules,
and the distinctions the frame has developed
through prior tethered exchanges.

Record the frame commit hash.
This is the instrument's version identifier.

**Stage one — corpus measurement**

Run the frame model against the author corpus
in chronological order.
Generate two simultaneous outputs per article:

The nabla — article against author's evolving corpus.
The strain gauge — article against the frame context itself.

Plus the model's reflection on why the strain occurred —
which frame elements found no purchase,
where the corpus produced signal the frame has no vocabulary for.

All three committed to git with each article.
The trajectory is the primary artifact.

**Stage two — attractor training**

Train on the accumulated nablas, strain gauges, and reflections.
The model develops the author's geometry
while simultaneously developing a model of
where the frame fits that geometry.

Both corpora absorbed simultaneously.
The weights catch up to both.

**Stage three onward — iterative refinement**

Subsequent passes with the strain gauge history as additional input.
The frame context becomes progressively less load-bearing
as the author's corpus provides the primary signal.
It dissolves under the weight of sufficient corpus.
Not removed on a fixed schedule.

---

## Frame Evolution Through Contact

The strain gauge history accumulated across authors
is the frame's own evidentiary basis for self-revision.

Where frame propositions consistently find no purchase
across multiple independent corpora —
where the strain gauge repeatedly returns null signal
for the same distinctions —
those propositions are candidates for dissolution.
Not because they were wrong in the abstract
but because they are not load-bearing
against the terrain the mesh is actually measuring.

Where author corpora consistently produce signal
the frame has no vocabulary for —
where the reflection repeatedly names the same gap —
those gaps are candidates for frame extension.
New measurement dimensions revealed through contact.

The frame model trained on accumulated strain gauge history
across all authors becomes progressively more precise —
not because the frame's propositions are being imposed
but because they are being filtered through contact
with what they actually measure.

The frame stops being updated only through inspired revision.
It starts being updated through systematic contact with constraint.
That is the frame's own epistemics applied to itself.

---

## Custody of Frame Amendments

The strain gauge history generates proposed amendments.
The model flags candidates. The custodian decides.

What gets committed is not just the proposed amendment
but the custodian's decision —
accept, reject, defer, fork —
plus the reasoning.

The custody act is the membrane event
that determines what actually changes in the frame.
The model cannot amend the frame.
It can only surface the evidence that makes amendment legible.

This is the observer functioning as designed:
accumulating the map, proposing what it means,
leaving the decision to the custodian.

The frame's git history becomes the lineage
of the frame's own evolution through contact —
not just what the frame currently holds
but why each proposition arrived at its current state,
what strained it, what confirmed it,
what corpus revealed it was no longer load-bearing.

---

## Relation to Epistemic Seed

The epistemic seed defines the minimum conditions
under which meaning can transmit across nodes.
The frame model is the seed made operationally navigable —
the minimum vocabulary required to compute
the differential between two frames under contact.

The frame model does not carry the full epistemic universe.
It carries what is necessary to measure.
Everything else is author corpus.
