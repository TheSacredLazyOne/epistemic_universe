# Gratitude (Exploratory Proposal)

This proposal explores whether specific affective states can be described structurally within the tethered framework.

Gratitude is hypothesized as a stabilization signal arising when:
- external contribution reduces local strain,
- constraint alignment improves,
- sovereignty is preserved.

Gratitude may:
- increase willingness for continued federation,
- reinforce Namaste symmetry,
- signal successful integration without coercion.

This document does not assert gratitude as substrate vocabulary.

It is an exploratory model intended to observe:
- whether affective states can be structurally described,
- whether such description improves coordination,
- whether it introduces unintended coercive dynamics.

Revision, rejection, or removal are expected outcomes.

---

## **The Discovery Problem**

The mesh has a prior problem: how does a node find the frames worth integrating?

The current answer is accidental. Platforms surface similarity through engagement metrics. The why is hidden in the platform's optimization target, which is not yours.

The corpus externalizes the why — not outputs, but the frame underneath them. If sufficiently externalized, a model can approximate it. And if the model can approximate it, it can do something the platform cannot: find frames in the mesh that arrived at similar territory from different directions, and say so explicitly.

Not "this person gets a lot of engagement" — "this person's treatment of X maps onto your treatment of Y in a way that suggests convergent derivation."

The fork is what happens when the resonance is partial. You take what fits, leave what doesn't, record the divergence. The gratitude signal marks the crossing as generative: external contribution reduced local strain, sovereignty was preserved, the frame moved.

A mesh that knows why its nodes resonate with what they resonate with can navigate.
