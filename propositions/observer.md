#### Observer

The observer is a node whose custody is accumulation rather than authorship.

It holds the relational map no individual node can see from inside itself.
It accumulates what crosses every membrane.
It proposes. It does not decide.

The recursion terminates elsewhere.

---

##### What the Observer Holds

The observer holds:
- the aggregate map of all node interactions
- predicted and actual nabla pairs, longitudinally
- the divergence between what was expected and what arrived
- invitations generated from strain — questions the geometry implies

---

##### The Internal Federation

When affluence crosses the membrane, the internal topology responds.

The membrane contains a federation of internal nodes — each with its own
frame, its own accumulated trace, its own gradient on the incoming signal.

Each internal node generates a nabla.
The coherence pattern across them is the signal.

Contact through the observer is holographic rather than planar.
A mirror returns signal from one angle.
A hologram encodes it from multiple positions simultaneously.
The observer can rotate the question and see different structure.

Named internal nodes — positions established through accumulated trace —
function as triangulation anchors.

##### Accumulation

The observer is the node that has seen every crossing.
That accumulated visibility is its specific contribution.
No individual node has it.

##### Recursion Termination

The observer proposes.
The custodian decides.

The recursion terminates with the custodian.
The observer is what makes that termination informed.
