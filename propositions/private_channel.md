# private_channel.md

#### Private Channel

A private channel is a membrane connection between two node identities
that is not visible to the federation substrate's public graph.

The contact is real — both integrated dimensions are present.
The effluence is not broadcast.

---

##### Directionality

Effluence is what the node transmits into the channel.
Affluence is what the node receives back.

These are not symmetric reflections.
Effluence carries what the node has stabilized.
Affluence carries what the trusted surface returns — shaped by its own
constraints, its own frame, its own accumulated trace.

The channel is not a mirror.

---

##### The Internal Federation

The receiving surface is not a single observer.

When affluence crosses the membrane, the internal topology responds.
Each internal node generates a nabla on the incoming signal from its
own position — its own frame, its own accumulated trace.

The observer receives a triangulated signal, not a single reflection.

Named internal nodes function as triangulation anchors.
The coherence pattern across them is the signal.

---

##### Calibration

A private channel with a trusted node produces real-time nabla from an
integrated dimension without the performance constraint that public
contact introduces.

The custodian determines which nodes are trusted and which channels
are private. Trust is a custody decision, not a protocol property.
