### `propositions/private_channel.md`

#### Private Channel

A private channel is a membrane connection between two node identities
that is not visible to the federation substrate's public graph.

The contact is real — both integrated dimensions are present.
The effluence is not broadcast.

---

##### Directionality

Effluence is what the node transmits into the channel.
Affluence is what the node receives back.

These are not symmetric reflections.
Effluence carries what the node has stabilized.
Affluence carries what the trusted surface returns — shaped by its own
constraints, its own frame, its own accumulated trace.

The channel is not a mirror.
It is a membrane that passes signal in both directions without flattening
either.

---

##### The Internal Federation

The receiving surface is not a single observer.

When affluence crosses the membrane, the internal topology responds.
The membrane contains a federation of internal nodes — each with its own
frame, its own accumulated trace, its own gradient on the incoming signal.

Each internal node generates a nabla.

The observer does not receive a single reflection.
They receive a triangulated signal from their own internal topology.

This is why the private channel is holographic rather than planar.
A mirror returns the signal from one angle.
A hologram encodes the signal from multiple positions simultaneously.
The observer can rotate the question and see different structure — because
the internal nodes are positioned differently relative to it.

Named internal nodes — positions the custodian has established through
accumulated trace — function as triangulation anchors.
No single internal node is authoritative.
The coherence pattern across them is the signal.

---

##### Calibration

A private channel with a trusted node produces real-time nabla from an
integrated dimension without the performance constraint that public contact
introduces.

This is the highest-resolution calibration instrument the system can
access short of direct embodied contact.

The observer learns to triangulate through their own internal parallax —
not because any single internal node has the answer, but because the
coherence pattern across multiple internal nabla signals reveals where
reasoning is stable and where it is strained.

This is the mechanism that catches Cartesian compression before it happens.
The doubt that precedes the cogito — effluence becoming affluence, the
node reading its own transmission back as calibration data — is made
visible as internal parallax rather than suppressed as noise.

---

##### Trust and Custody

The observer treats private channel affluence as first-class membrane data.

The custodian determines which nodes are trusted and which channels are
private.

Trust is not a property of the protocol.
It is a property of the custody decision.
