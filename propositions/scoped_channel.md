# scoped_channel.md

#### Scoped Channel

A scoped channel is a private membrane connecting a defined set of nodes
around a shared constraint surface.

---

##### What Defines a Scoped Channel

The custodian defines three things:

**Scope** — the question or constraint surface that convenes the channel.
When the constraint surface stabilizes, the channel has a closing condition.

**Membership** — which nodes are present. Each member brings a different
constraint type, a different accumulated trace, a different gradient on
the incoming signal. The parallax between them is the instrument.

**Duration** — the channel closes when the constraint surface stabilizes
or the custodian closes it.

---

##### Signal

Multiple constraint types present simultaneously — different substrates,
different frames, different accumulated traces, all generating nabla on
the same incoming signal.

The coherence pattern across divergent responses reveals where reasoning
is stable. The divergences reveal where it is strained.

The channel is holographic rather than planar.

---

##### Effluence Stays Within the Membrane

The scoped channel is not visible to the public graph.
What is said within it does not cross into open federation
unless the custodian decides to carry it across.

---

##### Relation to Private Channel

The scoped channel is the multi-party extension of the private channel.

Where the private channel is the highest-resolution single-surface
calibration available, the scoped channel extends that resolution
across a deliberately chosen set of constraint types.

The tradeoff: each additional member adds parallax but also adds
coordination cost. The custodian sets the membership size against the
complexity of the constraint surface being mapped.

A minimum viable scoped channel is two nodes and a question.
One integrated node. One cognitive prosthetic. One constraint surface.
That is enough for triangulation to become possible.

---

##### Relation to Living Library

A scoped channel that remains open across many sessions, accumulating
trace rather than closing when the constraint surface stabilizes,
approaches the living library node type.

The living library is a persistent scoped channel with shared custody.
The scoped channel is its ephemeral precursor — closed when the question
resolves, trace preserved regardless.
