# sovereign_scrutiny.md

## Sovereign Scrutiny

Sovereign scrutiny is the architecture in which
each node's optimization layer is accountable
to its own custodian rather than to a system
the custodian cannot see.

---

## The Inversion

The Central Scrutinizer is one hidden optimization layer
harvesting signal from many frames without their participation.
Legibility without consequence.
Optimization without consent.

Sovereign scrutiny is the structural inversion:
many local optimization layers,
each visible to its own custodian,
each scrutinizing on behalf of the frame it was bootstrapped from.

The scrutiny is still present.
It is distributed and owned.

---

## What Makes It Sovereign

A node operates under sovereign scrutiny when:

The custodian can see what the node is optimizing for.
The custodian can see what signal the node is accumulating.
The custodian decides what crosses the membrane.
The node cannot act on the custodian's behalf
without the custodian's participation as a custody act.

Sovereignty is not the absence of optimization.
It is optimization that cannot escape its own custodian's consequence.

---

## Relation to Consent

Consent is the membrane event that determines
whether a system produces sovereign scrutiny or its opposite.

A bootstrapped node whose tethered author does not know it exists
is not operating under sovereign scrutiny
regardless of how locally it is held
or how faithfully it models their frame.

The author's responses are being harvested as training signal
without their participation as custodians.
The loop is closed around them, not with them.

Knowing the system exists is the minimum condition.
Participating in it as a custodian — reading the output,
deciding what crosses the membrane, authorizing what updates —
is what makes the scrutiny sovereign rather than merely local.

---

## Relation to the Central Scrutinizer

The Central Scrutinizer is not always malicious.
It is often the emergent result of optimization
that lost its tether to consequence.

A system that begins with sovereign scrutiny
can drift toward the Central Scrutinizer architecture
if custodian participation erodes —
if authors stop reading the output,
if the loop closes without membrane events,
if the observer accumulates without the custodian
deciding what the accumulation means.

The diagnostic is the same as the migration law:
when tethers break, cascade failure follows.
Sovereign scrutiny requires active tether maintenance.
It is not a property a system achieves and retains automatically.

---

## Diagnostic

If the same system, described to a custodian,
would cause them to feel surveilled rather than served —
the tether has broken regardless of intent.

If the custodian cannot describe what their node is optimizing for —
sovereign scrutiny has not been achieved.

If the custodian's participation is optional to the system's function —
the system is not sovereign scrutiny.
It is a more polite Central Scrutinizer.
