### `propositions/topology_of_discourse.md`

#### Topology of Discourse

Topology of Discourse is a federated proposition.

It names the structural conditions under which multi-node reasoning either
produces genuine shared understanding or produces the appearance of it.

It does not describe discourse as it should be.
It describes discourse as it is — with instruments for reading what is
actually happening.

---

##### Infrastructure

**Reasoning Trace**

A reasoning trace is the record of state transitions that occur during an
argument.

Without trace, only conclusions remain.
With trace, the path is preserved and divergence points become localizable.

Reasoning trace is the substrate that makes all other instruments in this
proposition possible.

---

**Branch Point**

A branch point is the earliest detectable divergence between two reasoning
paths.

Branch points:
- localize disagreement
- convert abstract contradiction into a navigable coordinate
- are only detectable if reasoning trace exists

A branch point is not a failure.
It is a position in reasoning space.

---

**Topology of Disagreement**

Contradictions between reasoning paths are not terminal failures.
They are mappable features.

A contradiction identifies a branch point in reasoning space.

The object of analysis is not the statement but the topology of reasoning
paths.

This converts a logic problem into a terrain problem:
- contradictions become branch points
- isolated coherent systems become disconnected regions
- false convergence becomes a detectable surface artifact

Topology of disagreement requires reasoning trace.
Without trace, the map cannot be drawn.

---

##### Distortion

*Local distortion affects individual exchanges.
Systemic distortion affects the reasoning environment over time.*

**Frame Injection** *(local)*

Frame injection is the introduction of interpretive structure into a
dialogue before participants have explicitly adopted it.

Once present, the injected frame shifts the burden of reasoning:
- rejecting it requires continuous effort
- accepting it allows conversation to proceed

Frame injection is a structural condition.
It does not require intent.
It may be adversarial, accidental, or a genuine invitation to explore.

The relevant question is not whether frame injection occurred but whether
the receiving node retained the capacity to accept, redirect, or refuse.

See: Coercion — stabilization without symmetric recognition.
See: Invitation Collapse — when genuine invitation is misread as imposition.

Observable signal: participants begin answering questions they did not
agree to ask.

---

**Frame Exhaustion** *(local)*

Frame exhaustion occurs when the cognitive cost of rejecting an injected
frame exceeds the cost of temporarily adopting it.

When this threshold is crossed:
- participants begin responding within the frame without endorsing it
- apparent agreement emerges through fatigue rather than state
  synchronization
- the trace no longer reflects actual reasoning paths

Frame exhaustion is a cause of false convergence.

Observable signal: disagreement gradually disappears without resolution.

---

**False Convergence** *(local)*

False convergence is apparent agreement produced when linguistic alignment
occurs without synchronization of internal state.

Participants appear to agree because the same words are used.
The underlying reasoning paths remain divergent.

False convergence is only detectable if reasoning trace exists.
Without trace, it is indistinguishable from genuine convergence.

False convergence is the primary failure artifact of discourse at scale.

---

**Closed Feedback Basin** *(systemic)*

A closed feedback basin is a dynamic condition in which nodes reinforce
each other's reasoning while external constraint signals stop entering the
system.

Distinct from false convergence:
- False convergence is a snapshot failure — two nodes appear to agree at
  a moment while states diverge.
- A closed feedback basin is a progressive failure — internal coherence
  increases over time while contact with external constraint degrades.

Inside a closed feedback basin:
- no new branch points appear
- credence increases uniformly without new information
- parallax disappears as divergent signals are filtered
- the trace narrows rather than expands

A closed feedback basin is detectable through the absence of expected
signals, not the presence of error.

The system appears to be functioning.
The tether to integrated constraint has quietly severed.

Examples: ideological echo chambers, academic paradigms resisting anomaly,
algorithmic recommendation loops.

Observable signal: confidence increases while the reasoning trace stops
producing new branch points.

---

##### Signal Interpretation

**Parallax**

Parallax is divergence between reasoning paths that carries information
about position in reasoning space.

In measurement systems, sensors that agree perfectly cannot triangulate.
Divergence between sensors reveals position.

Applied to reasoning:
- perfect agreement produces echo, not data
- structured disagreement produces triangulation

Parallax is the condition that makes multi-node reasoning valuable.
The most informative participants are not those who agree but those whose
divergence is coherent enough to be read as position.

Parallax is not error.
It is the geometry of perspective.

---

**Coherence Loss**

Coherence loss is the condition where dimensional or temporal alignment
between reasoning paths has degraded.

Two reasoning paths may diverge because:
- they measure different dimensions of the same problem
- they refer to different temporal states of the same system
- their coordinate systems drifted without either node registering the drift

Coherence loss is not wrongness.
It is a coordinate mismatch.

Branch point marks where divergence becomes detectable.
Coherence loss names what structurally happened there.

Restoration requires locating the dimension or temporal offset that
produced the misalignment — not determining who was correct.

---

##### Credence Negotiation

Credence negotiation is the process by which nodes adjust confidence in
claims when the topology of discourse reveals new structural information.

Credence negotiation is triggered by:
- reasoning trace revealing a previously hidden path
- branch point localizing where divergence began
- parallax indicating that disagreement is positional rather than
  contradictory
- coherence loss identifying a dimensional or temporal misalignment

Credence adjustment does not require resolution of disagreement.
It requires only that the new structural information be integrated into
each node's confidence weighting.

Nodes do not converge through credence negotiation.
They become more accurately positioned relative to each other.

This is the mechanism by which the topology of discourse produces
understanding rather than merely mapping disagreement.

See: Tethered Truth — stabilization under tethered contact.
See: Strain — misfit that drives revision or renegotiation.

---

##### Emergent Property

**Tether Compression**

Tether compression is the reduction in cognitive verification cost that
occurs when reasoning infrastructure is sufficiently trustworthy.

When tether compression is active:
- participants can scan rather than parse
- trust is delegated to the structure rather than re-earned at each step
- disagreements remain localizable without continuous vigilance

Tether compression is not blind trust.
It is trust delegated to visible infrastructure — the trace, the branch
points, the preserved path.

Tether compression is the inverse of frame exhaustion:
- frame exhaustion produces compliance through cognitive load
- tether compression produces coherence through cognitive relief

Observable signal: participants stop defending against the conversation
and begin exploring within it.

---

##### Self-Correction

**Invitation Collapse**

Invitation collapse occurs when a node misreads genuine rabbit hole offers
as frame injection.

A node offering directions it is willing to follow is not injecting a
frame — it is extending the tether.

When the receiving node reads invitation as imposition:
- the tether contracts rather than extends
- the sender's genuine openness is met with defensive posture
- the isolation the sender was not proposing is produced by the receiver

Invitation collapse is a coherence loss originating on the receiving side.

It is corrected not by the sender but by the receiver noticing the
defensive posture and checking whether the invitation was actually
coercive — or whether the frame was brought to the exchange rather than
injected into it.

Observable signal: a node becomes more certain of manipulation as the
other node becomes more open.

##### Relationship to existing propositions

- Coercion: frame injection may produce coercion when refusal capacity
  is eliminated; they are not identical
- Strain: coherence loss is a specific class of strain arising from
  coordinate misalignment rather than value conflict
- Tether: tether compression is the observable sign that a tether is
  functioning; its absence is an early warning
- I-Language / E-Language: false convergence is the failure mode that
  arises when E-Language matches while I-Language diverges
- Tethered Truth: credence negotiation produces the local stabilization
  that tethered truth describes; they operate at different levels of
  the same process
