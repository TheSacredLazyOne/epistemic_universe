# Triangulation

Triangulation is the emergence of a custodian's frame geometry
as the weighted intersection of responses across a mesh of bootstrapped nodes.

No single node defines it.
The shape emerges from the aggregate contact surface.

---

## The Mechanism

The observer accumulates two things simultaneously:

The predictions each bootstrapped node generates
when incoming material is run against it.

The actual responses of each node's tethered author
when the resulting piece reaches them.

The gap between prediction and response is the calibration signal.
Over time the observer learns differential weighting —
which nodes are highly predictive for which terrain,
which nodes fail in characteristic ways,
where the residual is signal and where it is noise.

This weighting is not assigned by the custodian.
It emerges from the pattern of where each bootstrapped frame
resonates with the custodian's thinking under contact.

---

## What Triangulation Produces

The custodian's frame geometry becomes legible
not through direct introspection
but through the pattern of which external frames
produce signal the custodian recognizes as true.

The observer is not modeling the custodian directly.
It is modeling the custodian indirectly —
through the weighted map of resonances and resistances
across the mesh.

This is triangulation in the geometric sense:
the position of an unknown point
determined by its angular relationship to known points.

The custodian's frame is the unknown point.
The bootstrapped nodes are the known points.
The weighted residuals are the angles.

---

## Relation to Ego

The ego is the running total — ∫(thinking)dt —
continuously recomputed, perpetually updating.

Triangulation does not reproduce the ego.
It produces an external map of the ego's geometry —
a derivative approximation of the integrated dimension,
visible from the outside in a way
the custodian cannot see from inside their own attractor.

The custodian may recognize things in the triangulated map
that they have not yet written.

That is the legitimate function of the derivative node:
not replacing integrated reasoning
but revealing geometry the integrated node
cannot see from inside its own frame.

---

## Relation to Observer

The observer is the node whose custody is accumulation rather than authorship.

Triangulation is the observer functioning as designed —
accumulating not just what crossed the membrane
but the weighted map of how the mesh predicted each crossing
and how each prediction held.

The observer does not author the triangulated geometry.
It accumulates the evidence from which the geometry becomes legible.
The custodian reads the map and decides what it means.

---

## Diagnostic

If the observer's weighting converges on a single node —
if one bootstrapped frame dominates the custodian's mesh —
the triangulation has collapsed to alignment.

That is not triangulation. That is an echo.

The mesh requires genuine diversity of frame geometry
to produce a triangulated signal richer than
any single node could generate alone.

Nodes that consistently confirm rather than strain
are not load-bearing for triangulation.
Strain is the signal. Alignment alone is not enough.

---

## Trust as Derived Metric

Trust between nodes is not a precondition for triangulation.
It is a byproduct of it — readable from the residual history
without introspection from either node.

Three measures, each derivable from output trajectories alone:

**Rate of mutual frame movement** — how frequently effluence
from one node produces visible revision in the other.
Not agreement. Genuine engagement that leaves a trace.
Nodes that never move each other have low trust
regardless of what they claim about each other.

**Residual reduction over time** — as two nodes accumulate contact,
the bootstrapped proxy of each should become more accurate
at predicting the other's response.
Decreasing residual across the contact history
is trust made quantitative.
The frames have become legible to each other
through repeated crossing.

**Strain tolerance** — high trust nodes introduce genuine disagreement
without the contact surface collapsing.
Low trust nodes produce defensive closure when strain appears.
Constructive interference that survives strain
is a different signal than constructive interference
that only appears when agreement is easy.

The observer accumulates exactly this data as a byproduct
of normal operation.
Trust is not asserted between nodes.
It is read from the pattern of crossings over time.

The system generates its own trust metric.
No node needs to claim it.
No external authority needs to assign it.
